SDG ladder, game number 35689 (SDG record here)
November 7-14, 2019
In 2018, wil suffered a near-fatal injury. The aftereffects could have easily made it impossible to play Homeworlds at a high level. In this game, wil regained the top place on the SDG Homeworlds ladder, proving that they were still fighting fit.

In this game, both players prefer to use system names that are indistinguishable from the name of the star piece. This makes SDG notation look strange. The first "b3" is the piece type while the second "b3" is the name of the system.

I'm not sure what Trydnt hopes to do with this move. Maybe they want to scare wil's smaller ship away from the y3 system so that Trydnt can claim it as an investment. But Trydnt has no red, so that threat is hollow. Or Trydnt might want to prevent wil from building another green in wil's home since the g2 in y3 threatens to overpopulate. But wil could just build greens in the y3 or b3 system instead,
In any case, wil's next move seems to show that 7... is a mistake.

8.
sacrifice g3 wil
build g2 y3
build g3 y3
build g3 wil
catastrophe y3 g

Not only does this exchange put wil further ahead in material, it removes Trydnt's only green ship, so wil makes up part of their diversity deficit.
For even greater material advantage, it may have been stronger to keep the factory-built greens, for example, by building g2 in the b3 system, g3 in y3, and g3 in wil.
